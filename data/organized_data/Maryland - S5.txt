Something you might not know about me is...


the deep-seated passion that has been burning with me since I was 7 years old. Since then, badminton has been my best companion. Engaged in intensive training, I set my sights on representing my school on the badminton court and seizing every opportunity to attend a vigorous competition. But my aspirations reach far beyond our school's courts. Driven by an insatiable desire to push my limits, I have a grand vision: becoming the state champion of badminton in my college years. Badminton is not just a sport; it embodies my dream. 


Because we know that diversity benefits the educational experience of all students, the University of Maryland values diversity in all of its many forms.  This includes (but is not limited to) racial, socio-economic, gender, geographical, and sexual orientation.  We are interested in hearing about your own individual life experiences.  In a few sentences, will you please describe how you have learned, grown, been inspired or developed skills through one or more components of diversity. (650 characters)


Living in a country where Chinese, Japanese, and Taiwanese cultures intersect, and transitioning from a local school to an American school, with a wholly different learning environment and language, presented formidable challenges for me. Yet, amidst the challenges, I’ve learned to be a compromiser, finding the middle ground between two seemingly opposing styles, and positioning myself in my comfort zone. I’ve now immersed myself by setting iPhone language into English, reading my favorite badminton news in English, and mentally rehearsing my speaking while taking a shower. I have learned the process of adaptation from diverse cultures.
