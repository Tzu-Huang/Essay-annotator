Content: I have been interested in hydroponic systems on Mars ever since I explored the notion in my school’s science magazine. Space exploration is not only a milestone in scientific progress but also a wise precaution for longterm human survival. However, among the top concerns is the Martian environment, which is devoid of water and nutrients. This inspires me to conduct research into food production on Mars.

As Mars holds vast amounts of ice, I would have a team of hydrologists evaluate the feasibility of water extraction methods on Mars. I would also have a team of biologists analyze soil samples to determine whether nutrients found in the Martian environment could fully supply the hydroponic system without relying on imports from Earth. Another challenge is the limited availability of energy on Mars, which necessitates the implementation of energy-efficient systems. I would collaborate with a design team to maximize crops’ efficient exposure to LED lighting, and Martian sunlight if an evaluation from a team of physicists demonstrated the potential for sunlight to substantially contribute to crops’ energy demand. This project would certainly take more than a semester, but I know that Northwestern would be the place to pursue it.
