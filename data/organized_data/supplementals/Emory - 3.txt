What academic areas are you interested in exploring at Emory University and why? 
My interest in Biology started to grow when my brother shared his gross but fascinating thoughts about his bowel movements. He imagined it as a massive army collectively pushing a giant monster out of the gate. From then on, this idea stuck in my head, and I began to pay attention to every detail I felt in my body, creating scenarios to explain them. 
My curiosity deepened the first time I uncovered the mystery under our skin while conducting a perfusion on rodents in the lab. The tiny heart palpitates, and the moment I felt its rhythm, I realized that biology is like our closest stranger; it is a part of us, yet we don’t know everything about it. 
Although I’m drawn to becoming a front-line provider, conducting research remains one of my greatest passions. At Emory, the dual degree program especially excites me, as it allows me to develop in-depth biological knowledge while also gaining hands-on clinical experience through the School of Nursing. Additionally, I look forward to exploring research opportunities, particularly the Shepherd Lab, which focuses on the development of the enteric nervous system (ENS), as well as research collaboration in the School of Nursing at Emory.
