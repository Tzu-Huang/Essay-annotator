Tell a story from your life, describing an experience that either demonstrates your character or helped to shape it. *
Journey In the Ocean of Music (610)
Switching schools is hard, and I stumbled at first. My transition to an international high school perplexed me since I had studied in a traditional Chinese-based education system since elementary school. When I had the chance to join the new school’s orchestra, I saw it as an opportunity to integrate myself. I could use my skilled ears to be someone.
I joined the orchestra as a violist, despite only knowing the violin. At the onset, I was overly aware of my limited skill in playing the viola. Like a salmon that had lost its way, I often bowed the wrong direction and felt isolated. Consequently, the conductor told me that he thought I lacked dedication. Trapped in a maze of insecurity, my fingers would tremble as I played. For half a year, I played in self-imposed isolation and with hesitation. 
As the semester went on, the burden of tasks grew heavier, and the conductor doubled the amount of pieces to be practiced. Sensing something was amiss, my viola teacher told me to stay after a typical Tuesday afternoon practice. Although I knew he just wanted to talk, I started to stutter. Afraid of getting punished, tears streamed down my face.
My viola teacher, Mr. Yang, spoke with an unwavering tone. He calmed me and said I needed to shed the self-imposed pressure weighing me down. Just go for it, don't overthink. Though tears continued to flow, I felt a sense of relief.
Breaking free from the shadow of shyness that I had cast over myself, I listened intently when the first chair violist advised me to loosen my wrist. Despite the ongoing challenge and isolation that surrounded me – my peers practicing the conductor's assigned pieces, leaving little room for connection – I persevered. I spent hours studying YouTube videos, refining the speed and precision of my fingerings. I practiced my bowings, tirelessly constructing the flexibility of my wrist with each repetition. In moments of respite, I dived into the musical sheets, swimming in every note and committing to memory each conductor’s notation on those sheet music. As a result, I started to gain the courage and strength to mute the discouraging voices inside my head, and my confidence began to return. 
The date was January 17, 2023, my birthday. Filled with determination to conquer my shyness, I tightened my bow and tuned my A string just before I began the chair test. Shivers ran down my spine, sweat glistened on my forehead, and uneasiness gnawed at my fingertips. In the end, despite a few inaccurate notes and missed bowings, I earned the position of head chair of the viola section.
Through the challenging journey of overcoming my shyness and embracing my true self, I've come to recognize the transformative power of continuous self-reflection. It's an ongoing process, one that requires constant effort and openness to learning. I've learned to embrace my shortcomings, understanding that they are not just hurdles but essential stepping stones for personal growth. Acceptance and bravery have become my dependable allies on the road to self-evolution, allowing me to face present and future challenges with a growing sense of resilience. These lessons continue to shape my path towards personal growth and success, serving as a crucible where my identity is ever-evolving. From a once-shy student to a leader guiding the viola section during the national orchestra competition, my journey continues. As I enter university, I hope to carry this mindset forward, recognizing that the process of self-discovery is ongoing, and each challenge is an opportunity for further development. It remains a powerful catalyst, propelling me forward academically and personally, just as it did in the realm of music.
