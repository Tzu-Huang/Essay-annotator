To persuade a normal individual to commit murder under social pressure takes only about 72 minutes. "The Push" experiment undergoes a series of psychological manipulations and social pressures, ultimately challenging participants to push a person off a rooftop. It's fascinating because it reveals how swiftly social conformity can lead people to act against their moral values, exposing the malleability of the human mind as individuals adapt to societal norms.  However, in real life, when faced with pressures, it is essential to pause and reflect on our actions, asking ourselves, "What am I doing?"
