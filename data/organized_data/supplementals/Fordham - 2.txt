“Why do you want to attend Fordham University, and how will you contribute to our academic and campus community?”
At Fordham, I aspire to contribute my experience to the Frank J. Petrilli Center for Research in International Finance. As my father was an avid investor, we would frequently investigate economic environments in East and Southeast Asia; this, combined with my experience as a long-term resident in Taiwan, fostered my unique expertise and perspective towards Asian financial markets. Furthermore, I hope to bring my research abilities and intense work ethic to the research center. I was my group’s main researcher during the Wharton Global Youth program, gathering all necessary information within the first few days of the week our group has. I even contacted an engineer at Google; even though I place efficiency in high regard, what matters most is that our information quality went above and beyond the minimum requirements. In the end, we received an award for the best poster, a kind of distinction I hope to replicate at this university. 
Additionally, I am eager to collaborate with fellow journalists in The Observer. I am currently the chief editor of our school’s science magazine club. At peak times, I would review two articles a day on average, making sure to return them with comment-filled margins within a week. This is not to mention that I am also the co-founder of the club; I actively recruited members, provided ideas for new articles, and often planned overnight with my peers to discuss deadlines, reader feedback, and content difficulty. Even though managing the two positions may be tiresome, seeing members improve in their penmanship and seeing readers indulging themselves in our hard work immediately washes my fatigue away. Though I do not anticipate holding a leadership position within my first year, I am excited to help those in the club with my experience and passion for journalism.
