Prompt: How will opportunities at Purdue support your interests, both in and out of the classroom? 
Content: I love our planet. I want to understand how it functions and geek out over all its facets – from my rock collection to the tectonic plates. However, I do not pursue knowledge for knowledge’s sake alone; I intend to build a career fighting the effects of climate change.
The Environmental Geosciences major offers a clear academic path to my goal. I see great potential in large scale data analysis and climate modeling, so I am very interested in advanced courses like ‘Global Change Modeling’ (EAPS 42000) and Data Analysis Techniques In Earth And Atmospheric Sciences (EAPS 50900). Moreover, my field research during the Geography Olympiad showed me the value of combining classroom theory with hands-on experience, so I want to enrich my learning by participating in studies on extreme weather or climate dynamics. I am particularly interested in assisting Dr. Huber with his work on climate modeling and simulating changes within the Earth system as that is exactly the kind of research I would like to do some day.
Purdue’s extracurricular offerings speak to me as well. As an avid outdoorsman, I am exceptionally drawn to the Purdue Outing Club. Having traversed all over Taiwan’s mountains, I would love to make new international friends while exploring America’s stunning landscapes. On a different track, I am also interested in the Purdue Equestrian Team. Horse-riding is a completely novel experience to me, but that is what college is all about. Whichever way I may go, I know Purdue has got me covered.
