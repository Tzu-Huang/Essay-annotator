Describe the people, places, events, or circumstances that have shaped the individual you are today. How could you contribute to the diverse and inclusive community at Santa Clara University?
Growing up in Taiwan, a country rich in cultural diversity—from indigenous traditions to influences from China, Japan, and Southeast Asia—has shaped my appreciation for diverse perspectives. Experiencing these distinct cultures firsthand taught me the importance of adaptability and respect for differences, shaping my worldview and approach to collaboration.
As the leader of my high school’s iGEM team, I had the honor of guiding our team to win the 2024 iGEM Grand Prize. Leading a multidisciplinary group with members focused on research, marketing, and public health initiatives required balancing diverse perspectives to create a cohesive and effective approach to dengue prevention. Coordinating efforts with local public health officials and international researchers gave me a profound understanding of the value of empathy, open communication, and inclusiveness in achieving shared goals. These experiences reinforced the importance of uniting individuals with different backgrounds and expertise to address complex challenges.
At Santa Clara, I hope to contribute to the university’s inclusive community by engaging in the Ciocca Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurship. I am excited to bring together students from various backgrounds to work on entrepreneurial projects that serve diverse communities. I believe that by fostering a space where everyone’s perspectives are valued, we can create innovative solutions that address the unique needs of different populations. My experiences navigating multicultural dynamics and leading a diverse team at iGEM will help me contribute positively to the collaborative and inclusive spirit at Santa Clara University.
