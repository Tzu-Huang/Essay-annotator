After the Grand Jamboree, I was both flattered and shocked when Laurent contacted me in December, inviting me to join GEMS-Taiwan for the 2024 iGEM competition. Despite his casual mention of this possibility in Paris, I had taken it as a joke. Receiving the invitation was a surreal moment, especially knowing the high regard in which GEMS-Taiwan was held. Surprisingly, none of my new teammates knew who Laurent was, as the team had new members each year. We still had other instructors—Jakie for Wetlab, Timmy for Drylab, KJ for software, and Jessie for Human Practices—but Laurent’s absence was felt keenly by me.

Despite initial challenges, the team environment was incredibly welcoming. During the topic selection phase, where each member proposed ideas, I was delighted when someone chose to join my topic, which eventually became the second topic in our selection process. This gesture of support made me feel included and motivated. Personally, I became more open and communicative, no longer afraid to share my thoughts. Professionally, I drew on my experiences from WIST_iGEM to offer practical suggestions, particularly for our human practices team.

However, my teammates, especially Sophie, who was incredibly extroverted and friendly, encouraged me to stay calm and assured me everything would be fine. Special thanks to Sophie, whose friendliness helped me bridge the gap with my new teammates and find my voice within the team.

Though Laurent is no longer part of the team, his initial belief in me set me on this path. The experiences and connections I’ve gained have been invaluable, both personally and professionally. My journey from WIST_iGEM to GEMS-Taiwan has taught me resilience, the importance of a supportive environment, and the power of mentorship. These lessons will guide me as I continue my journey in synthetic biology.
