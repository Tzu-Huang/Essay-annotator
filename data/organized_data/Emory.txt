Emory University has a strong commitment to building community. Tell us about a community you have been part of where your participation helped to change or shape the community for the better. 
My community has excellent healthcare facilities and insurance, yet this abundance sometimes results in patients overusing resources and nurses facing burnout. As soon as I reached the minimum age for hospital volunteering, I joined the Ton-Yen Hospital team. Although I lacked professional knowledge, offering empathy required no formal training. I still remember an elderly woman trembling after hemodialysis as her husband searched helplessly for support. In my broken Taiwanese Hokkien, I offered a warm pink blanket. Her faint smile, in return, filled the ER with a warmth that words couldn’t express. Moments like this reminded other volunteers and staff that compassion could ease workloads and restore humanity to a fast-paced medical system. 
With the knowledge I gain at Emory, I hope to extend this compassion beyond hospital walls by joining student organizations like the Atlanta Pediatric Cancer Organization, supporting families in Atlanta with the same empathy that first inspired me.
