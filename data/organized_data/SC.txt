Think about an ethical dilemma in society that you care about, either locally or globally. How can an education at Santa Clara help you address this challenge?
I am concerned about balancing profit with public health accessibility in the biotech industry. Companies often prioritize profits, leaving underprivileged communities without essential healthcare solutions. I believe businesses can operate both profitably and responsibly to ensure life-saving innovations reach everyone.
An education at Santa Clara University, with its focus on ethics and social responsibility, will help me address this challenge. Through the Leavey School of Business, courses like "Business Ethics and Social Responsibility" will teach me to develop strategies that combine financial sustainability with social impact. The Miller Center for Social Entrepreneurship will offer practical experience in designing ethical business models for public health issues.
With these tools, I aim to lead biotech initiatives that bridge innovation and accessibility. My goal is to create ventures that prioritize both profitability and positive societal impact, making health solutions accessible regardless of economic barriers. Santa Clara's education will prepare me to drive change, aligning public health with ethical business practices.
