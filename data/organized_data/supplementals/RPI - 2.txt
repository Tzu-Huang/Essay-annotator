Content: EMERGENCY NOTICE: Without a faculty advisor, HCAS Times will soon be shut down.
I had just become the editor-in-chief of the newspaper club, HCAS Times, when I got the email. But I was determined to revive the club.
Our previous advisor had unexpectedly left the school right before the start of the semester. That meant we had less than two weeks to find a new advisor. I took rapid action, phoning and sending emails to the club members and various school staff, asking for their help recommending and reaching out to a suitable teacher. Within a few days, we had a hit: I contacted a new English teacher who was more than happy to help.
After settling the advisor issue, another issue arose during the school year regarding our publishing medium. Previously, the school had always printed out hard copies for us to hand out to the student body and parents; however, this year, the school would no longer do so. To resolve the situation, I sat down with the school management team to request reopening the printing and drafted a proposal to our Parent-Teacher Association to support it, but unfortunately, both appeals were denied.
All the pressure was on me, but I knew that I could not let all our members' hard work go to waste. I immediately gathered my club members with computer science-related experience and, combined with my own expertise, developed a framework for our online platform. Within a month, we were publishing articles online. Over the next year, it was rewarding to see this close community of three members expand to more than twenty, cooperating with the school administration and student organizations to deliver the most relevant news to the HCAS community.
