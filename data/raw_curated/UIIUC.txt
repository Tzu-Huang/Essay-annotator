Describe your personal and/or career goals after graduating from Illinois and how your selected first-choice major will help you achieve them. (150)

I love our planet. I want to understand how it functions and geek out over all its facets – from my rock collection to the tectonic plates. However, I do not pursue knowledge for knowledge’s sake alone; I intend to build a career fighting the effects of climate change. Tackling such a complex problem will require expertise in a variety of intersecting fields, like urban planning, computer science, and environmental science. Geography and GIS’ interdisciplinary orientation thus provides a great starting point for my academic development. For example, I am inspired by Dr. Sivapalan’s fusion of sociology and hydrology, and I am excited to learn more about this approach. Moreover, the GIS program will teach me to model and analyze the effects of climate change through large scale geographic data. This will empower me to develop new methods of combatting these effects and help maintain our one and only home.
