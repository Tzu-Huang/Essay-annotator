Prompt: Emory University has a strong commitment to building community. Tell us about a community you have been part of where your participation helped to change or shape the community for the better.

Content: When I was born, our house was surrounded by farmlands. As I grew up, our community was redeveloped into a technology district, and I enrolled in the newly built international school on a scholarship. Unlike my friends’ parents, who all had advanced degrees, my parents never graduated from high school or learned English. However, my school was incredibly supportive; peers volunteered to practice English with me, and teachers provided hours of extra help.

When I reached high school, I desired to give back by making knowledge more accessible. I co-founded the Science Magazine, helping aspiring writers make contemporary discoveries understandable to middle schoolers. I also founded Investing Lab and taught eager students how good trades stem from a clear sight of the market and its interplay with the overarching economy. Disadvantaged students need resources and confidence to succeed, and I will always try my best to pass them on.
