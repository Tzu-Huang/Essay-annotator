Prompt: Briefly describe what prompted you to apply to Santa Clara University. Based on what you know about SCU and our Jesuit mission to educate citizens and leaders of competence, conscience, and compassion, how do you envision your life at SCU and beyond? (200 words)

Content: My parents never attended college. As a first-generation student, I've always sought advice and mentorship from whomever was willing to share it. Hours of free help from programmers on StackOverflow and GitHub helped me get past coding bottlenecks. Seeing how they helped inexperienced developers grow, at no cost but for the love of expanding the coding community, I found that sharing competence is compassion.
These values of sharing knowledge and collaborative learning became my values. I devoted an hour per day over grades 8-10's summer break answering others' questions on Stack Overflow. I became a teacher's assistant in my middle school, helping students who were falling behind. I've found great joy helping younger students succeed, not only academically, but also providing emotional support. I hope to continue to share competence at SCU, where conscience and compassion are core values. I would participate in Drahmann Center's no-cost tutoring, join the school of engineering's Mentor Collective and further share my experience in writing research papers by participating in the HUB Writing Center. Just as I've gained from and returned much to online coding communities, SCU provides many opportunities to enrich my own and others' college experience by embracing a competent and compassionate culture.
